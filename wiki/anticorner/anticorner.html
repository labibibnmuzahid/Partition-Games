Anti-corner

Integer Parition Combinatorial Game

Let $\left( \lambda_1, \lambda_2, \lambda_3, \ldots, \lambda_n \right)$ be a parition of $n$, where $\lambda_1 \geq \lambda_2 \geq \cdots \lambda_n > 0$. The game centers around "Anti-corners": in each move, an Anti-corner $(i, j)$ needs to be selected, and upon selection the hook associated to that cell would get deleted. An Anti-corner belongs to one of the followings:
  1. Degenerate Anti-corners: Only two specific cell consists this set — the most top-right cell and the most bottom-left cell.
  2. Proper Anti-corners: An proper anticorner is a square in the Young diagram that sticks out at a “dip’’ in the outline: it has no square directly to its right and no square directly below it, so you can delete that square together with everything to its right in the same row and everything below it in the same column and still be left with a valid partition shape.
Alike previous games, Anti-corner is played between two players; each gets one move at a time; and the one who makes the last move wins.
