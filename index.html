About

Rhodes College Summer Fellowship Program '25

This project is being developed under the supervision of Professor Gottlieb. Soumtiro and Aayan are crafting this web application to aid Prof. Gottlieb and his team's research on combinatorial games, making these mathematical concepts approachable to everyone.

choose your game

LCTR

Remove the left column or top row.

play now rules

CRIM

Remove any column or row. The remaining pieces are merged.

play now rules

CRIS

Remove any column or row. The remaining pieces remain separated.

play now rules

Sato-Welter

Remove the hook rooted at any square. The remaining pieces are merged. A hook rooted at a square is that square together with the squares directly below it and directly to the right of it.

play now rules

RIT

Remove a positive number of squares from the terminus of any row, provided the result is still a Young diagram.

play now rules

Corner

Remove any nonempty set of corners. A corner is a square that is both at the end of a row and the bottom of a column.

play now rules

Anticorner

Remove any anticorner. An anticorner is the rightmost column, the bottommost row, or a set of squares in the shape of an L that connects two adjacent corners.

play now rules

Consecutive Corner

Remove any nonempty set of consecutive corners.

play now rules

CRPM

The row player can remove any row and the column player can remove any column. The remaining pieces are merged.

play now rules

CRPS

The row player can remove any row and the column player can remove any column. The remaining pieces remain separated.

play now rules

CRIT

Remove a positive number of squares from the terminus of any row or any column, provided that the result is still a Young diagram.

play now rules

SICC

Remove a nonempty set of consecutive corners that include either the top right corner or the bottom left corner (or both).

play now rules
