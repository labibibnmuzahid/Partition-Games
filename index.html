About

Rhodes College Summer Fellowship Program '25

This project is being developed under the supervision of Professor Gottlieb. Soumtiro and Aayan are crafting this web application to aid Prof. Gottlieb and his team's research on combinatorial games, making these mathematical concepts approachable to everyone.

partition games

explore the world of combinatorial games with integer partitions

choose your game

LCTR

When it's their turn, the user can remove either the left column, or the top row.

play now rules

CRIM

The user may remove any row or column - but the two divided parts would get merged after each move.

play now rules

CRIS

Moves are same as CRIM, but no merging in this case. Fragments parts will be independent, meaning a move in one dividend wouldn't impact others.

play now rules

Sato-Welter

Remove a hook (an upside-down L shape) from any square: all squares to the right in the row, and all squares below in the column.

play now rules

RIT

Some cells of a row can be removed as a move as long as it has same or more number of cells than the row below it.

play now rules

Corner

In a uniformed partition, a corner is the last cell of a row; however, in case of consecutive rows with equal length, the cell only from the last row is considered as a corner. In this game, a random subset of corners gets removed from the partition.

play now rules

AntiCorner

Moves take place just like Sato-Welter; however, the user chooses an Anti-corner, instead of a random cell.

play now rules

Continuous Corner

A Corner variant where players must select consecutive ranges of corner pieces.

play now rules

CRPM

Partizan version of CRIM where Player A can only remove rows and Player B can only remove columns.

play now rules

CRPS

Partizan varition of CRIS, where Player A controls rows, and Player B controls columns in fragment paritions.

play now rules

CRIT

An enhanced adaptation of RIT; in addition to “row-comparison” removal, a new move column comparison, too, will be legal: some cell of column $c_i$ can be removed as long as it is greater than or equal to $c_{i + 1}$.

play now rules

SCC

SCC – Strict Continuous Corner; each move will begin with either the topmost or bottom most corner. Then, a sequential subset of corners will be chosen for removal.

play now rules
