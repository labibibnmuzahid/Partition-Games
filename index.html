Each game is a different rule for breaking a partition apart. Hover a card to feel the depth — then play.

Left Column, Top Row

Remove the left column or the top row. A classic impartial game with a Sprague–Grundy AI.

Column Row Immediate Merge

Remove any row or column; the remaining fragments merge back together after the move.

03 — the why

built for research, made for play

Partition Games supports ongoing work in combinatorial game theory at Rhodes College — turning Sprague–Grundy values, minimax search, and partition arithmetic into something anyone can explore. Developed by Soumitro and Aayan under the Summer Fellowship '25, supervised by Prof. Eric Gottlieb.
