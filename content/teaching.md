# Teaching

GRASP turns combinatorial game theory into something you can **see, touch, and play** — a tool for the classroom as much as for research.

## How to use these games in class

- **Start from a partition.** Every game here is played on a Young diagram, so students can connect the board they see to the integer partition behind it.
- **Play, then explain.** Let students play a few rounds against the perfect-play AI, then turn on **analysis mode** to reveal the Grundy values and the winning moves.
- **From Nim to chess.** The iChess games show how familiar pieces become impartial games: the Rook is two-pile Nim, the Queen is Wythoff's game, and the Knight has no closed form at all.

## Concepts the games illustrate

1. **Sprague–Grundy values** and the mex rule.
2. **P-positions and N-positions** (losing vs winning positions).
3. **Normal vs misère** play conventions.
4. **Sums of games** and how independent regions combine.
