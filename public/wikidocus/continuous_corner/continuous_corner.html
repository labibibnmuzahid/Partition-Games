Continuous Corner

Integer Parition Combinatorial Game

Let $[r_1, r_2, r_3, \ldots, r_i]$ be an integer partition of a positive integer $n$ in descending order, where $\forall x \in \left(1, 2, \ldots, i\right), r_x \geq 1$. Continuous Corner is a variant of the Corner game designed to be played between two players, where the game board will be a Young diagram, representing a partition with previously documented properties. When it's their turn, the player will select a consecutive subset of corner pieces to remove from the board. Like in Corner, corner pieces are the last cells of rows (following the consecutive grouping rule), but unlike Corner, players cannot select arbitrary subsets - they must choose consecutive corners only. The player who makes the last move wins.
